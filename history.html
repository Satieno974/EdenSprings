Our History

The Chaplaincy ministry in the Adventist Church began at Battle Creek College (ca.1875) and expanded shortly after into Battle Creek Sanitarium and other Adventist health care facilities. World War II prompted a few Adventist ministers with the vision to venture into the military services as chaplains. Nearly one hundred Adventist clergy now serve as military chaplains, providing pastoral care for Adventists in the military services. The first Adventist chaplain for a correctional institution started serving in the California State prison system in 1959. Adventist Chaplaincy Ministries (ACM) was established as a service for the General Conference in 1985 and became a department in 1995. In Kenya, Adventist chaplaincy ministry (ACM) started as universities, schools and colleges’ ministry, which was a department in the East Africa Union. Pastor Artiks, who was the chaplain at Maxwell academy, was the first ACM director of the union, later Ken Bushnell became the first ACM Departmental director in the East Africa Union, followed by Pastor Squerra. It is during Squerra’s tenure that the universities groups started regular worships and later organized as church. It was also in his tenancy that the fields and conferences started ACM department and even deployed some pastors to the organized universities churches as universties chaplains. The Chaplaincy department in Edens Springs SDA church is fairly a new department started off in the year 2023 with the leadership of Nelly Mayaka. The department keeps on growing by training and equipping new chaplains to serve. The first cohort of chaplains were training by Caleb Adera and consisted of seven chaplains: Elder Jared Ombasa, Steve Abeka, Nelly Mayaka, Roseline Maraga, Catherine Kiage, Lillian Ombasa and Chrispina Machuki.
