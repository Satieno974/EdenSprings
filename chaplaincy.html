About Us

Eden Springs SDA Church Chaplaincy provides spiritual, emotional, and holistic care in diverse settings, sharing Christ’s love and compassion with communities.

Our Chaplaincy

Welcome to the Eden Springs ADA Chaplaincy Ministry, serving the East Nairobi Field with our motto of "Chaplains - Saved to Serve" and our slogan of "Chaplaincy - Holistic Ministry." Our dedicated team consists of church members who have undergone specialized training to become chaplains, equipping them to provide comprehensive spiritual support and care. While distinct from pastoral roles, our chaplains are uniquely prepared to offer specialized spiritual guidance in various settings. We serve in diverse areas including learning institutions, communities, correctional facilities, healthcare institutions, armed uniform institutions and workplaces, addressing the spiritual, emotional, and physical needs of those we encounter. Adhering to the high standards established by the Seventh-day Adventist Church for chaplaincy ministries, we strive for excellence in our service. Our chaplains undergo professional training similar to other specialized fields, ensuring they are well-prepared to meet the challenges of this calling. If you feel drawn to this meaningful work, we invite you to contact us and learn more about our training and endorsement process. Join us in our commitment to holistic ministry as we extend Christ's love and compassion to our community.

Our vision

Compassionate care & concern

Our Mission

To provide Christ-centered psychosocial-spiritual care and support across diverse settings within Nairobi East Field

Our purpose

To extend Christ's compassion and the Adventist message beyond our church walls, fulfilling the gospel commission as outlined in Matthew 25:31-46. We are trained and commissioned to complement the church's evangelistic efforts by providing specialized spiritual care, support, and guidance in diverse settings and restricted areas where traditional church outreach may not reach. Our chaplains serve as bridges between the church and the wider community, touching the lives of those who may have no formal Connection with organized religion. Through care, concern, dialogue, counseling, and support, we aim to meet people where they are – in workplaces, educational institutions, healthcare facilities, armed forces, and crisis situations.
We are committed to:
1. Offering compassionate spiritual and emotional support in specialized
2. Providing crisis intervention and counseling to individuals and families in need.
3. Fostering interfaith understanding and dialogue in diverse environments.
4. Promoting ethical decision-making and character development in various professional contexts.
5. Supporting the holistic well-being of individuals facing challenging life circumstances.
6. Representing Christ's love and the Adventist message through practical service and empathetic presence.
